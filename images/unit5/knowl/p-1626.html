The bebop scale (known more specifically as the “bebop dominant” scale in jazz theory texts) is identical to the Mixolydian scale except is has an added ↑\(\hat{7}\) . The added chromatic note (↑\(\hat{7}\) ) occurs in descending passages (from \(\hat{8}\) –\(\hat{7}\) –♭\(\hat{7}\) ) as a chromatic passing tone. The bebop scale is most often used over the dominant 7th chord. In the most rudimentary form of improvising, one can use the bebop scale in descending eighth notes beginning on the downbeat of a measure, starting on the root, 3rd, 5th, or 7th.
