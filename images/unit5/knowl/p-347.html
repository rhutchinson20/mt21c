Dominant function (abbreviated “dom.”): The \(\left.\text{V}\right.\) and \(\left.\text{vii}^{\circ}{}\right.\) (chords containing the leading tone \(\hat{7}\) and supertonic \(\hat{2}\) ) tend to progress to tonic (\(\left.\text{I}\right.\)). Special note: The \(\left.\text{I}\middle/\text{5th}\right.\) chord has dominant function when it resolves to the \(\left.\text{V}\right.\) chord, as in the third chord from the end of the “Star-Spangled Banner.”
