5th may be raised or lowered (♯5 or ♭5, which can also be written with pluses and minuses as \(\left.\text{}{+}\right.\)5 or –5) and can be respelled enharmonically (the ♯5 of a C7♯5 could be written as an A♭ instead of G♯) for ease of reading because the chromatic spelling agrees with B♭ (the 7th of the chord)—it is easier to read a chord where the accidentals agree (all sharps or all flats).
