Item 3.

Approach the ending octave or unison by contrary motion; one of the parts will end with \(\hat{7}\) –\(\hat{8}\) while the other ends with \(\hat{2}\) –\(\hat{1}\) .

  1. If the example is in G mixolydian, D dorian, or A aeolian, use a sharp to create the leading tone (F♯, C♯, and G♯ respectively).
